# Real-World Pathways™ Code of Conduct for Business Partners

**Version 1.0 — Effective: 2026**
**Issued by: WealthWise Kids LLC, operating as Real-World Pathways™**

---

## PURPOSE

Real-World Pathways™ exists to connect students with real-world career experiences in a safe, structured, and equitable environment. Every Business Partner who joins the platform makes a commitment — not just to their industry, but to the young people who will learn from them.

This Code of Conduct defines the standards of behavior, professionalism, and ethics we expect from every Business Partner organization and all personnel who interact with youth through the RWP platform.

---

## SECTION 1 — YOUTH-FIRST COMMITMENT

### 1.1 The Youth Come First
Every decision made during a RWP-facilitated experience must prioritize the **safety, dignity, and well-being** of youth participants above all other considerations — including business operations, convenience, or cost.

### 1.2 Treat Youth with Respect
All youth participants must be treated with dignity and respect regardless of race, ethnicity, gender, religion, national origin, disability, sexual orientation, socioeconomic status, or academic standing. Dismissive, demeaning, or discriminatory treatment of youth is grounds for immediate removal from the platform.

### 1.3 No Exploitation
Youth may not be asked to perform work that primarily benefits your business without providing genuine educational value. This includes tasks that would normally require paid employees, that are outside the scope of the agreed experience, or that have no clear career development purpose.

---

## SECTION 2 — PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

### 2.1 Language & Tone
- Use professional, age-appropriate language at all times
- Profanity, sexual language, or inappropriate humor is never acceptable in the presence of youth
- Do not discuss politics, religion, or controversial social topics in ways that could be divisive or alienating to youth participants

### 2.2 Physical Conduct
- Physical contact with youth must be limited to brief, professional interactions (e.g., a handshake)
- No hugging, physical affirmation, or extended physical contact with youth participants
- Maintain appropriate physical distance; do not position yourself in isolated spaces with individual youth

### 2.3 Two-Adult Rule
- Whenever possible, two (2) background-cleared adults should be present when youth are on-site
- If a one-on-one interaction is necessary (e.g., a virtual job shadow), it must take place with the youth's school/organization contact copied on communications or actively aware of the session
- Never be alone with a single youth in a private, non-public, or enclosed space

### 2.4 Electronic & Digital Communication
- All digital communication with youth must be professional, purposeful, and related directly to the RWP experience
- Communications must be visible to or copied to the youth's school/organization coordinator
- Do not connect with youth on personal social media accounts, personal phone numbers, or any platform not sanctioned by their school or organization
- Do not take photos or video of youth without express written consent from their parent/guardian and school/organization

### 2.5 Gift Giving
- Incidental, group-appropriate items are acceptable (e.g., branded materials, snacks for a group visit)
- No individual gifts of monetary value to youth participants
- No gift cards, cash, or items of personal significance

---

## SECTION 3 — HONESTY & TRANSPARENCY

### 3.1 Accurate Representation
- Business Partners must accurately represent their organization, the type of experience being offered, and what youth can expect during the experience
- Do not overstate credentials, experience, safety measures, or capacity
- Do not advertise positions, mentors, or programs that are not actually available

### 3.2 Disclosure
- Business Partners must immediately disclose to RWP any change in their organization that affects their ability to host youth safely, including:
  - Personnel changes affecting approved Youth-Contact Supervisors
  - Insurance lapses or coverage changes
  - Active legal proceedings related to youth safety, workplace misconduct, or employee conduct
  - OSHA violations, state safety citations, or workplace incidents

### 3.3 No Hidden Agendas
- Youth experiences must not be used as recruiting pipelines for labor, unpaid work, or commercial activities that primarily benefit the business
- Business Partners may not use youth contact information obtained through RWP for marketing, sales, or recruiting outside of the platform's intended purpose

---

## SECTION 4 — EQUITY & INCLUSION

### 4.1 Equal Access
- Business Partners must offer experiences equally to all youth referred through the RWP platform, without discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, socioeconomic status, disability, religion, or other protected characteristics
- Selection criteria for competitive experiences (e.g., internships) must be clearly defined and applied consistently

### 4.2 Accessibility
- Business Partners are encouraged to make reasonable accommodations for youth with disabilities to participate in experiences
- Virtual or modified formats should be offered when physical accessibility is a barrier

### 4.3 Cultural Awareness
- Business Partners should be aware of and respectful toward the cultural backgrounds, values, and communication styles of youth they serve
- RWP serves diverse communities across the United States; cultural competency is an expectation, not an extra

---

## SECTION 5 — REPORTING & ACCOUNTABILITY

### 5.1 Report Concerns Immediately
If any Business Partner personnel observes conduct that violates this Code of Conduct — whether by another employee, a youth participant, or a school/organization representative — they must report it through the RWP incident reporting system or contact RWP's Trust & Safety team directly.

### 5.2 No Retaliation
No Business Partner may retaliate against any individual for reporting a concern in good faith. Retaliation — including canceling an experience, withdrawing a partnership, or taking adverse action — is itself a violation of this Code and grounds for removal from the platform.

### 5.3 Self-Reporting
Business Partners are expected to self-report incidents, concerns, and changes in their compliance status. Self-reporting is always viewed more favorably in RWP's review process than discovered violations.

---

## SECTION 6 — CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATIONS

RWP reserves the right to take the following actions in response to Code of Conduct violations:

| Severity | Examples | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Minor | Inappropriate language, isolated unprofessional behavior | Written warning; mandatory additional training |
| Moderate | Privacy violation, unauthorized communication with youth, failure to report a near-miss | Temporary suspension; corrective action plan |
| Serious | Pattern of misconduct, unauthorized one-on-one contact, data misuse | Extended suspension; probationary status |
| Severe | Abuse, assault, exploitation, fraudulent registration | Immediate permanent removal; cooperation with law enforcement |

## ACKNOWLEDGMENT

By registering as a Business Partner on the RWP platform, you confirm that:

- You have read and understood this Code of Conduct
- You will ensure all personnel with youth contact are aware of and comply with these standards
- You accept that violations may result in suspension or permanent removal from the platform
