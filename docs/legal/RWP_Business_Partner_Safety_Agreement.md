## OVERVIEW

This Business Partner Safety Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between **WealthWise Kids LLC, operating as Real-World Pathways™** ("RWP," "Platform," or "We") and the business, employer, or organization registering to participate on the Platform ("Business Partner," "Partner," or "You").

By completing registration and receiving a **RWP Verified Partner™** designation, you agree to all terms set forth in this Agreement. This Agreement governs your participation in the RWP platform and your interactions with youth, schools, and organizations connected through it.

---

## PART 1 — WHO THIS COVERS

This Agreement applies to:

- The Business Partner organization as a whole
- All employees, supervisors, volunteers, and representatives of the Business Partner who will have **direct contact** with youth participants (students, ages K–12)
- All physical locations, virtual environments, or remote settings in which youth experiences take place under the Business Partner's coordination

---

## PART 2 — VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

Before being approved as a RWP Verified Partner™, Business Partners must submit the following:

### 2.1 Business Legitimacy
- Employer Identification Number (EIN) or equivalent documentation
- Active business license or registration in the state(s) of operation
- Company website or verifiable digital presence

### 2.2 Insurance Documentation
- Certificate of General Liability Insurance with a minimum coverage of **$1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate**
- Business Partners hosting youth for in-person, extended, or unsupervised experiences are strongly encouraged to carry **Abuse & Molestation Coverage** (coverage amounts vary; RWP will provide guidance upon request)
- Workers' Compensation Insurance (required if youth are classified as paid interns or employees in your state)

### 2.3 Background Screening
- All designated **Youth-Contact Supervisors** (any person who will have direct, supervisory, or unsupervised contact with youth during a RWP-facilitated experience) must complete:
  - State criminal history records check
  - FBI fingerprint-based background check (or equivalent federal check)
  - National Sex Offender Registry check
  - Child abuse and neglect database search (where available in your state)
- Background checks must be conducted through a RWP-approved or FCRA-compliant consumer reporting agency
- Results are reviewed by RWP's Trust & Safety team; RWP reserves the right to deny or revoke Verified Partner status based on findings

### 2.4 Reference Verification
- A minimum of two (2) professional references attesting to your organization's character, reliability, and youth-serving practices

---

## PART 3 — SAFETY STANDARDS & OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Supervision
- At least one (1) approved, background-cleared adult supervisor must be present during **all** youth experiences
- For one-on-one or small-group experiences (fewer than 5 youth), a minimum ratio of **1 adult per 3 youth** is required
- Youth must never be left unsupervised, alone with a single adult in a non-public setting, or placed in an environment without a clear supervision plan

### 3.2 Environment Safety
- Business Partner locations must be free of unreasonable hazards and compliant with OSHA standards applicable to your industry
- Youth must not be assigned to tasks involving hazardous materials, heavy machinery, or conditions prohibited under federal or state child labor laws
- Business Partners must provide a written **Workplace Safety Summary** identifying any site-specific hazards and the controls in place

### 3.3 Communication with Youth
- All communication with youth participants must occur through school/organization-approved channels
- Business Partners may not initiate direct personal contact with youth outside of a supervised, platform-sanctioned experience without express written consent from the youth's parent/guardian and the organization they attend
- Digital communication (texts, emails, social media) between Business Partner personnel and individual youth is prohibited unless reviewed by or copied to the youth's school/organization contact

### 3.4 Mandatory Reporting
- All Business Partner personnel with youth contact are considered **mandated reporters** for the duration of any RWP-facilitated experience
- If you witness or have reasonable suspicion of child abuse, neglect, or any threat to a youth's safety, you must:
  1. Report immediately to local child protective services (CPS) and/or law enforcement
  2. Notify RWP's Trust & Safety team within 24 hours via platform incident reporting
  3. Cooperate fully with any investigation

---

## PART 4 — YOUTH SAFETY TRAINING

### 4.1 Required Training
Prior to hosting any youth experience, the designated Youth-Contact Supervisor(s) at your organization must complete the **RWP Youth Safety Orientation** (approximately 2 hours, online, self-paced). This orientation covers:
- Age-appropriate interaction standards
- Boundary maintenance and one-on-one interaction policies
- Recognizing and responding to signs of distress, abuse, or disclosure
- Mandatory reporting obligations by state
- Incident documentation and reporting procedures on the RWP platform

### 4.2 Training Completion
- Completion is verified automatically through the RWP platform
- Training must be renewed **annually** or when a new Youth-Contact Supervisor is added
- RWP reserves the right to require additional training based on the type of experience offered (e.g., internships, mentorships) or following any incident

---

## PART 5 — PARENTAL CONSENT & PARTICIPANT DATA

- Parental/guardian consent for youth participation is the responsibility of the **school or organization** connecting through RWP; however, Business Partners must confirm that consent documentation is in place before any experience begins
- Business Partners may not collect, store, or use personal information about youth participants for any purpose other than coordinating the RWP-facilitated experience
- Youth data may not be shared with third parties, sold, or used for marketing purposes
- All data handling must comply with FERPA, COPPA (if youth are under 13), and applicable state privacy laws

---

## PART 6 — INCIDENT REPORTING

### 6.1 Reporting Obligation
Business Partners must report the following to RWP within **24 hours** of occurrence:
- Any injury sustained by a youth participant during a RWP-facilitated experience
- Any allegation or disclosure of abuse, harassment, or misconduct
- Any safety incident, near-miss, or emergency situation
- Any violation of the terms of this Agreement

### 6.2 Reporting Process
Incidents are reported through the Business Partner dashboard under **Safety & Compliance → Report an Incident**. RWP's Trust & Safety team will acknowledge receipt within 1 business day and initiate a review within 3 business days.

### 6.3 Consequences
RWP reserves the right to:
- Suspend or remove a Business Partner's Verified Partner status pending investigation
- Permanently revoke Verified Partner status based on substantiated findings
- Cooperate with law enforcement or child protective services as required

---

## PART 7 — ANNUAL RECERTIFICATION

Verified Partner status requires annual renewal, which includes:
- Confirmation that all designated Youth-Contact Supervisors have current background checks (no older than 3 years; high-contact roles require annual re-check)
- Updated Certificate of Insurance on file
- Youth Safety Training completion current for all active supervisors
- Acknowledgment and re-signing of this Agreement
- Declaration of any incidents or compliance issues from the prior year

RWP will send renewal reminders beginning **60 days before expiration**. Failure to complete renewal will result in suspension of the Verified Partner badge and removal from the active marketplace.

## PART 10 — TERMINATION

Either party may terminate this Agreement with **30 days written notice**. RWP may terminate immediately and without notice if:
- A Business Partner is found to have provided false information during registration
- A substantiated safety incident occurs involving youth harm
- A Business Partner fails to comply with mandatory reporting obligations
- Background check results indicate a disqualifying offense

Upon termination, the Business Partner's profile and Verified Partner badge are immediately removed from the platform.
