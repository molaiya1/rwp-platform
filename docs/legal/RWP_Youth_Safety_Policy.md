# Real-World Pathways™ Youth Safety Policy

**Version 1.0 — Effective: 2026**
**Issued by: WealthWise Kids LLC, operating as Real-World Pathways™**

---

## STATEMENT OF COMMITMENT

Real-World Pathways™ is built on a single belief: that every young person deserves access to safe, structured, real-world experiences that expand their understanding of what's possible.

That belief only holds if **safety is not negotiable**.

This Youth Safety Policy defines RWP's platform-wide standards for protecting youth participants and outlines the obligations of every party — Business Partners, schools, organizations, and RWP itself — in maintaining a safe environment for all experiences facilitated through the platform.

---

## SECTION 1 — WHO THIS POLICY PROTECTS

This policy applies to all **youth participants** connected through the RWP platform, defined as:
- Any individual under the age of 18 participating in a RWP-facilitated experience (site visit, job shadow, career panel, mentorship, internship, project partnership)
- Youth may be as young as kindergarten age (5 years old) for classroom-based or supervised group experiences
- This policy applies to **in-person, virtual, hybrid, and asynchronous** experiences

---

## SECTION 2 — SHARED RESPONSIBILITY MODEL

Youth safety on the RWP platform is a **shared responsibility** among three parties:

| Party | Primary Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **RWP Platform** | Vetting and verification of Business Partners; platform safety architecture; incident escalation; policy enforcement |
| **Business Partners** | On-site safety, supervision, and conduct during the experience |
| **Schools / Organizations** | Parental consent; student readiness; pre-experience preparation; post-experience follow-up |

No single party can guarantee youth safety alone. This model requires active participation from all three.

---

## SECTION 3 — PLATFORM SAFETY STANDARDS (RWP OBLIGATIONS)

### 3.1 Verification Before Visibility
No Business Partner profile is made visible to schools or organizations until the following have been verified:
- Business legitimacy (EIN, license)
- Insurance documentation (current and meeting minimums)
- Background screening completed for all designated Youth-Contact Supervisors
- Youth Safety Orientation completed
- Business Partner Safety Agreement signed

### 3.2 Trust Status Display
Every Business Partner profile displays a clear trust status:
- **RWP Verified Partner™** — All requirements met; active and compliant
- **Pending Verification** — Application under review; not yet active in marketplace
- **Verification Expired** — Annual renewal overdue; temporarily removed from marketplace
- **Suspended** — Active review or investigation in progress

### 3.3 Compliance Monitoring
RWP monitors Business Partner compliance on an ongoing basis through:
- Insurance expiration alerts (60-day and 30-day reminders)
- Background check renewal scheduling (annual or triennial based on role)
- Training completion tracking per supervisor
- Incident report review and follow-up
- Youth feedback surveys following each experience

### 3.4 Incident Response
When an incident is reported, RWP's Trust & Safety team will:
1. Acknowledge receipt within **1 business day**
2. Begin review within **3 business days**
3. Determine if temporary suspension is warranted
4. Coordinate with the school/organization and law enforcement as required
5. Issue a final determination within **10 business days** for non-criminal matters
6. For matters involving potential criminal conduct: immediate suspension and full cooperation with authorities, with no internal timeline constraint

## SECTION 4 — SAFE EXPERIENCE DESIGN

### 4.1 Experience Types & Risk Levels

Different experience types carry different inherent risk levels and require different levels of safeguards:

| Experience Type | Contact Level | Minimum Safeguards |
|---|---|---|
| Career Panel / Classroom Visit | Group, supervised | 1 background-cleared presenter; school staff present |
| Site Visit | Group, supervised | 1+ background-cleared adult on-site; signed safety summary |
| Job Shadow | Individual/small group, supervised | 1+ cleared supervisor; parental consent; school aware |
| Virtual Mentorship | Individual, distance | All sessions logged; school coordinator copied on comms |
| In-Person Mentorship | Individual, recurring | Annual background check; 2-adult rule encouraged; progress logs |
| Internship | Individual, extended | Background check; workers' comp if paid; parental consent; structured plan |
| Project Partnership | Group, ongoing | Project plan on file; supervisor cleared; check-in milestones |

### 4.2 Prohibited Activities
Regardless of experience type, the following are prohibited in all RWP-facilitated experiences:
- Youth left alone with a single adult in a non-public, enclosed, or private space
- Youth assigned to tasks involving hazardous materials, heavy machinery, or conditions restricted under child labor laws
- Youth asked to sign any document without their school/organization's knowledge and parental consent
- Youth data or contact information collected for purposes unrelated to the experience
- Any form of physical discipline, public humiliation, or coercive interaction
- Transportation of youth by Business Partner personnel without prior written parental and organizational consent

## SECTION 5 — MANDATORY REPORTING OBLIGATIONS

### 5.1 Who Is a Mandated Reporter
Under this policy, all Business Partner personnel with direct youth contact are treated as **mandated reporters** for the duration of any RWP-facilitated experience, regardless of whether your state law specifically classifies your role as such.

### 5.2 What Must Be Reported
The following must be reported to child protective services (CPS) and RWP:
- Reasonable suspicion or direct knowledge of child abuse (physical, sexual, emotional, neglect)
- A youth's disclosure of abuse or harm — even if they ask you to keep it secret
- Witnessing conduct by any adult (including colleagues) that could constitute abuse or grooming behavior
- Any situation in which a youth appears to be in immediate danger

### 5.3 How to Report

**Step 1 — Secure the youth** (if in immediate danger, call 911 first)

**Step 2 — Report to authorities**
- Call your state's child protective services hotline (numbers available in the RWP partner dashboard under Safety Resources)
- For national guidance: Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline: **1-800-422-4453**

**Step 3 — Notify RWP**
- Report through the Business Partner Dashboard → Safety & Compliance → Report an Incident
- Or email: **safety@realworldpathways.com**
- Include: what you observed, when, who was involved, what action you took
- RWP's Trust & Safety team will respond within 1 business day

**Step 4 — Do not investigate yourself**
- Do not confront the alleged abuser
- Do not promise confidentiality to the youth
- Do not pressure the youth for more details than they voluntarily share
- Preserve any communications, notes, or records related to the incident

### 5.4 Failure to Report
Failure to report a suspected abuse or safety incident is a violation of this policy and may constitute a criminal offense under state law. Business Partners with substantiated failures to report will be permanently removed from the RWP platform.

## SECTION 6 — YOUTH FEEDBACK & VOICE

### 6.1 Post-Experience Surveys
Following every completed experience, RWP sends a brief, age-appropriate survey to the youth participant's school/organization contact for distribution. Surveys ask:
- Did you feel safe during the experience?
- Did the adults treat you with respect?
- Was the experience what you expected?
- Is there anything you want to tell us about your visit?

Youth responses are confidential. Patterns or flagged responses trigger a Trust & Safety review.

### 6.2 Concern Escalation
Youth and their parents/guardians can report concerns about a RWP experience at any time through:
- Their school or organization coordinator
- RWP's Trust & Safety email: **safety@realworldpathways.com**
- Anonymous tip line (coming in Phase 2 of platform development)

### 6.3 Youth Are Believed First
RWP operates under a **youth-first disclosure policy**: when a youth reports a concern about an experience or an adult's conduct, that report is treated as credible and investigated promptly. Business Partners are not given an advantage in the review process simply because they are adults or paying partners on the platform.

## SECTION 7 — DATA PRIVACY & PROTECTION

### 7.1 What Data RWP Collects About Youth
RWP does not directly collect personally identifiable information (PII) from youth. All youth data flowing through the platform is managed at the school/organization level. RWP collects only:
- Aggregate participation counts (how many youth participated in an experience)
- Experience outcome data (provided in summary form by the school/organization)
- Post-experience survey responses (anonymized before reaching RWP)

### 7.2 What Business Partners May NOT Collect
Business Partners may not independently collect, store, or use:
- Youth names, contact information, or social media handles for any purpose beyond coordinating the experience
- Photos or video of youth without parental and organizational consent
- Academic records, grades, assessment results, or FERPA-protected data
- Demographic data or any data that would allow individual youth to be identified outside the platform

### 7.3 Applicable Law
All data handling is governed by:
- **FERPA** (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)
- **COPPA** (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act — applies to youth under 13)
- **State privacy laws** applicable to the Business Partner's state of operation
- RWP's own Privacy Policy (available at rwp-platform.vercel.app/privacy)

## SECTION 8 — POLICY UPDATES

This policy is reviewed **annually** and updated as needed based on:
- Changes in federal or state law
- Incident patterns identified through platform monitoring

- Emerging best practices in youth safety

Business Partners will be notified of material updates with at least **30 days notice** and must re-acknowledge the updated policy at their next annual renewal.
